Jennifer Graves, B.A.
  Marriage and Family Therapist
    Description/Role

Counselors who use brief solution-focused therapy or other techniques in working with couples and families. Over half of therapy is completed within 20 sessions. Marriage and family therapists have specific goals for their clients and therapy is designed with an end result in mind. They work with individuals, family groups or couples to help modify behaviors and perceptions, enhance communication and understanding, and prevent family and individual crises. They also can treat such mental disorders such as adult schizophrenia, affect disorders, child conduct disorders and substance abuse. Marriage and family therapists may also conduct research and teach in the field of human development and interpersonal relationships.

Requirements

  • Graduate training in an accredited program (48-60 hours of graduate study)
  • At least 2 years of clinical training, which in the state of Texas includes at least 3000 hours of clinical service
  • Successful completion of an examination prescribed by the Texas Marriage and Family Therapy Board

Job Conditions

Benefits: Benefits vary depending on where you work. If you work for a hospital or state agency there is a likelihood of receiving some sort of health insurance as well as retirement. If you are running a private practice, benefits are paid out of pocket. 

Pay Scale: Median earnings in 2000 were $34,660. The middle 50% earned between $27,970 and $44,320. The lowest 10% earned less than $22,770 and the top 10% earned above $44,320.

Hours: Often work flexible hours to accommodate families in crisis and working couples who must have evening and weekend appointments.

Professional Development

  • In the state of Texas licensed individuals must complete 15 hours of continuing education per year (3 hours of ethics every three years) from an approved board sponsor.
  • Some board approved sponsors include Children's Medical Center of Dallas, Texas Woman's University, Irving Family Advocacy Center, and Green Oaks Hospital.
