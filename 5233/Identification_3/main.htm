Identifying Researchers & Theorists

[Click on a passage below to learn the source.]

  1. His 1975 and 1979 publications on Uncertainty Reduction Theory posited that if uncertainty levels are high, strangers' verbal communication will decrease.
  2. Her Expectancy Violation Theory of 1978 was empirically shown to predict that when person A's behavior violates person B's expectation, there is a response. If the relationship or exchange is positive, the behavior (violation) will be interpreted positively, more positively than if it were expected. If the exchange between A and B is not going well, then B will react negatively when A's behavior violates B's expectation. (Classic example: if the job interviewer is already shaky, don't do anything unexpected.)
  3. This French intellectual and semiologist wrote of writerly and readerly texts in the 1973 The Pleasure of the Text. Briefly, writerly text challenges readers with new ideas, violating the status quo. Readerly text validates the status quo and may refer to any classic texts.
  4. He introduced American society to Groupthink in 1972. The theory proposes that high-cohesion groups of individuals may insulate themselves from alternative views and make decisions that reflect harmony and consensus regardless of the consequences. Such a group may assume that past successes make future plans fool-proof; the group may operate without a system of rules, norms, checks and balances, etc.
  5. This famous anthropologist theorized in 1973 that organizations have their own culture. The Cultural Approach interprets culture as shared meaning, spun as a web by man.
  6. Well known among family therapists, this person wrote the classic book entitled Peoplemaking. She emphasized the importance of caring and acceptance as healing elements. She focused on helping family members share feelings, affections, and love.
