Catalog Description:

Current research and issues in family sciences: emphasis on conceptual and theoretical frameworks in guiding research design and critical analysis of recent literature in family sciences. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours.

 

 

Course Objectives:

  1. Identify major conceptual frameworks and e mpirical techniques prevalent in studying families.
  2. Identify and critique selected theoretical and conceptual literature on selected family issues.
  3. Become a knowledgeable consumer of conceptual and empirical literature on families.
  4. Demonstrate skills in evaluating, synthesizing, and summarizing.
  5. Demonstrate in-depth understanding of selected theoretical and conceptual perspectives.

 

 

 

Course Requirements:

  1. Class attendance and participation. Attend all classes and participate in discussions and activities. 10% of grade.
  2. Content analysis of a journal. Select a scholarly journal that has published at least four issues across 2000 and 2001. Select four consecutive issues and analyze all articles and essays for conceptual or theoretical frameworks and for empirical approaches or techniques. Prepare a grid for display in class and submission (a sample will be shared at the first class) as well as a text summary of your analysis for submission. Th e summary should include a conclusion that you have drawn about the material published in the journal. The summary must not exceed 500 words. 15% of grade. (No partial credit)
  3. Serve as the discussant for a chapter or assigned reading. Consider carefully what activity or discussion will support your peers’ full unders tanding of the material and plan accordingly. You can assume that everyone will have read the material before class. 15% of grade.
  4. Contribute 500 to 1000 words of scrupulously clean research reporting to a class website on the issue of divorce. The site and topics will be conceptualized in the first four class meetin gs. 10% of grade.
  5. Review of research. Develop a “Comprehensive Review of Recent Research” on an approved topic. Use APA 5th edition as your style guide. Text of manuscript should be minimum 3500 words; reference list will extend beyond this length. Topic must be submitted by September 17 via e-mail or on paper. An outline or similar conceptualization of the paper must be submitted by October 29 at the latest. 30% of grade. Be prepared to present a short (!) overview of your topic during the month of November.
  6. Final exam. To be conceptualized in class. 20% of grade.

 

Policies and Procedures:

  1. Written work should be typed. The style manual for the American Psychological Association ( APA), 5th ed., should be used for citations, references, and manuscript style.
  2. Written work should be turned in according to class schedule. Grade deductions will be made for late work.
  3. I welcome e-mail submissions. Attachments should be in Microsoft Word. (Website submission can be in any html file.) I acknowledge every assignment with a message that “XXX was received in good order.” If you submit an assignment and do not receive this reply within a few days, you should assume that I did not receive your assignment. Due dates are firm regardless of server problems.

Family Sciences Diversity Policy:

 

The concept of diversity implies a total commitment to an open context that promotes equality, social tolerance, and the right to express different points of view. The Family Sciences Department practices and celebrates diversity and creates a culture characterized by a climate of inclusion rather than exclusion, where people are involved in working together for a common good. Diversity refers to the inclusion of all persons regardless of their differences. These differences include but are not limited to ethnicity, race, culture, gender, age, socio-economic status, religion, sexual orientation, and physical disability. The Family Sciences Department does not condone discrimination in any form and complies with Texas Woman’s University Non-Discrimination Policy. The faculty actively seek enrichment through the strength, power, and wisdom of diversity.
