  School Counselor
    Description/Role

School Counselors work to improve the school’s mental health climate through enhanced self-knowledge; interpersonal communication skills; responsible behavior and conflict resolution; decision making and achievement motivation; goal setting and career planning. The ultimate g oal for a school counselor is to help students develop effective life skills that will serve as a valuable asset for the rest of their lives.

Requirements

  • TEA Certification (EXCET examina tion)
  • 3 years teaching
  • MA/MS Counseling/Dev

Job Conditions

Benefits:

  • Teacher Retirement (TRS, 403B)
  • State Health Benefits
  • Life Insurance

Pay Scale: Range – $38,000 to $65,000 (escalates within this range with years of experience)

Hours:

  • A school counselor works from 195 days to 210 days a year (varies among school districts)
  • 2- to 3-month vacation per year
     

Professional Development

  • TEA Annual Conference
  • Staff development hours required per year by ISD
