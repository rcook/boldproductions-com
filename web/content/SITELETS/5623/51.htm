Carmen Rodriguez, B.B.A.
  Certified Play Therapist
    Description/Role

A Play Therapist helps troubled children cope with their distress using play as the medium of communication between the child and the therapist. Play therapy is an appropriate approach to counseling children because play comes naturally to children. Children under the age of 9 or 10 have not yet developed the abstract reasoning and verbal abilities to sit in a counselor’s office and articulate their thoughts and feelings.

Requirements

  • Masters degree from an accredited institution in a medical or mental health profession
  • 150 clock hours of instruction in play therapy
  • 2 years (2000 clock hours) of clinical experience
  • 500 hours of supervised play therapy experience

Job Conditions

Benefits:

  • Consultants to other mental health professionals or school districts.
  • Other options may include employment at an agency, hospital or school district.

Pay Scale: 65+ per hour. Salary will be in accordance with the approved Play Therapist salary schedule adopted by the prospective school district, agency, or hospital.

Hours: Schedules will be in accordance with the prospective clients, agencies, school districts, and hospitals in order to meet the needs and demands at hand.
