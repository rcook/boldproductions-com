Extension Agent
    Descriptio n/Role

Extension Agents are part of a statewide educational organization that is made available to the people in communities through the cooperation of the U.S. government, the state government, and the county government. The primary role of an Extension Agent is to inform and teach residents to strengthen families and support youth through workshops, seminars, community events, media, and technological tools.

Extension Agents work extensively with people and dedication to education and the commitment to build community strengths is essential. The Extension Ag ent couples personal initiative and creativity with organizational and interpersonal skills resulting in a unique opportunity for personal and professional growth.

Family and consumer sciences Extension Agents educate individuals, families, and various other groups and businesses concerning child development, nutrition, family economics, and general health topics.

4-H youth development Extension Agents work extensively with 4-H clubs and school programs to benefit youth ages 9-19. 4-H Agents also lead training programs to prepare adult and parent volunteer leaders.

Requirements in Texas (other states may vary)

  • Master’s degree in family and consumer sciences, education, or a related field or
  • Bachelor’s degree – provided applicant agrees to complete a Master’s degree within 7 years and additional criteria related to grade point average
  • Desire and ability to work with people from diverse environments
  • Able to work independently and interactively
  • Exhibit traits that encourage public trust
  • Effective teaching ability
  • Professional appearance
  • Vehicle for travel
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a valid drivers license

Job Conditions

Benefits in Texas (other states may vary):

  • Annual leave
  • Paid holidays
  • Study leave programs
  • Sick leave
  • Unemployment compensation and state workers’ compensation insurance
  • Group insurance and base life – employee coverage fully paid by employer contribution
    [Optional - Health, Dental, Life, Accident, Disability income, Long-term care
  • Retirement
  • (Texas) Teacher Retirement System of Texas
  • Social Security

Pay Scale in Texas (other states may vary):

  • Longevity pay increase of $240 per year for every three years of state service
  • Compensation can be increased with advanced degree combined with successful job performance
  • Compensation based on education, professional experience
  • Entry level compensation with Master’s degree $29,500 (Bachelor’s degree $27,500)
  • Base Plus Program
  • Professional plus Extension experience reach 6, 12, and 24 months
  • Increases total $3,900.

Hours: Willing to work necessary hours to fulfill job responsibilities; Some evening and weekends.
