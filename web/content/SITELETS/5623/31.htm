  Family and Consumer Sciences Teacher
    Description/Role

A Family and Consumer Sciences (FCS) teacher helps to prepare middle, junior, intermediate, and high school students for their future responsibilities of family and career. This is accomplished through lectures, purposeful classroom discussions, prepared worksheets and hands-on laboratory experiences as well as the guidance of student learning through Family, Career and Community Leaders of America (FCCLA) activities both inside and outside the classroom. In addition, FCS teachers often use technology such as personal computers to evaluate and record student progress, communicate with other educators as well as parents, retrieve educational updates, and guide students in research.

Requirements

  • Bachelor of Arts or Bachelor of Science and
  • Acceptable score on Examination for Certification of Teachers (EXCET)/Texas Examinations of Educator Standards (TExES) beginning Fall 2004, or
  • Emergency certification with a deficiency plan available through web-based distance education www.sbec.state.tx.us

Job Conditions

Benefits:

  • Teacher Retirement
  • State Health Insurance 
  • Seasonal employment opportunities
  • Student loans payment programs www.sbec.state.tx.us
  • Possible Benefits by school district:
    • Signing bonus
    • Bilingual stipend
    • Beginning teacher mentors
    • 401K
    • In house child care. 

Pay Scale: Texas state base $24,240 (Beginning) - $40,800 (20+ years) based on a 10 month contract plus district pay increase.

  • Annual increases 
  • Master's degree: +$500 - $1000 
  • Extra-curricular stipends vary

Hours: 10 - 11 months, varies by district and position

  • 40 hours, Monday - Friday plus preparation time
  • Seasonal holidays vary by district

Professional Development:

  • District professional development and required teacher in-service
  • Resources for FCS professional development:
  • Texas Education Agency www.tea.state.tx.us
  • American Association of Family and Consumer Sciences www.aafcs.org
  • Texas Association of Family and Consumer Sciences www.fcstat.org
  • Family, Career and Community Leaders of America www.texasfccla.org

Outlook:

By 2011, more than half of the Texas FCS teachers will be eligible for retirement creating many new opportunities.
