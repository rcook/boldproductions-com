Close the loop, close the gap: directives that ask for assessment cycles to be designed by faculty, staff, and administrators. The documentation of these efforts become the goal, which can be a solution or a new problem. Some samples are below, followed by red flags that accreditors say they find in documentation.
 
Bean-counting Research Class Report on email and discussion for a graduate class in research methods
Bean-counting Econ Class Report on email and discussion in a graduate course in family economics
Bean-counting Template Template for Instructors and TAs to report on communications with students
PGP Documentation Teaching Class Report on a graduate class in terms of Principles of Good Practice
.
Red Flags:
DL doesn't match mission / Admin cannot articulate

Target populations changed

Curriculum approval different for DL

Large class sizes expected

Classes have "synchronous" expectations

Only one method of assessment

Cookie cutter approach

Poorly designed discussion questions

Rapid turnover of adjuncts

Outdated course materials

Inadequate pedagogy [or andragogy]

Inadequate infrastructure
On-campus requirements for off-campus programs

Lack of a "known contact point"

Enrollment growth exceeds capacity

High DFW rates

.
